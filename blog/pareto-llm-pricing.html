Most LLMs are strictly dominated.

2026-01-26 3 min read Прочитать по-русски →

There are hundreds of LLMs on the market. Prices range from $0.025/M tokens (Gemma 3n) to $14/M (GPT-5.2 xhigh) — a 560× spread. Most of them shouldn't exist: too expensive for their intelligence, or too dumb for their price.

I pulled benchmark data from artificialanalysis.ai and plotted intelligence (a composite of benchmarks, with variants for coding, math, LiveCodeBench, GPQA) against price. Then drew the Pareto frontier — the maximum intelligence you can buy at each price point.

Anything below the frontier is strictly dominated: for the same price a better model exists, or for the same intelligence a cheaper one does.

(An interactive chart with five tabs — general intelligence, coding, math, LiveCodeBench, GPQA — and a filter by provider, belongs here. A site-styled version is being built; in the meantime the verdict is below.)

Verdict, by use case:

  • Phone / local. Gemma 3n E4B (Google): intelligence 6.3 at $0.025/M. Good enough for simple tasks, runs locally.
  • Weak GPU. DeepSeek R1 0528 Qwen3 8B: intelligence 16.4 at $0.068/M. Reasoning-capable for pennies.
  • Daily driver. DeepSeek V3.2 Reasoning: intelligence 41.6 at $0.315/M. Best price-per-intelligence among serious models.
  • High-stakes work. GPT-5.2 xhigh: intelligence 51.2 at $4.8/M. Current capability ceiling. (Arguably Claude Opus 4.5 belongs here; it doesn't land on the composite frontier, so the approach isn't perfect, but it orients you.)

Every model below that line is money you're overpaying.
