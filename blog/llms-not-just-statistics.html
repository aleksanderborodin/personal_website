The "LLMs are just statistics" argument is stuck in 2022.

2025-08-10 4 min read Прочитать по-русски →

There's a claim that LLMs are just advanced systems predicting the most likely next token given average text. I don't buy it.

2020 — GPT-3. A model that predicts the next token with decent accuracy. That's advanced statistics.

2022 — InstructGPT. Introduced RLHF — fine-tuned on human reactions to make responses "nicer". This doesn't continue text statistically — it continues it the way humans prefer.

2022 — ChatGPT. Not just text continuation — fine-tuned on curated chat examples, so continuing the chat means answering the user's question. Still statistics, but of a curated subset, not the whole internet.

2022 — PaLM 540B. Introduced Chain-of-Thought prompting (CoT). The model runs recursively, adding its own "thoughts" to the prompt. Still statistical at train time, but at inference the model now sees the user's prompt plus its own thoughts — so the first token it returns is no longer the statistically most-likely continuation of the user's question. It's the most-likely continuation of user-question + model-thoughts, which at non-zero temperature is a different thing.

2025 — DeepSeek R1. Introduced reinforcement learning for training reasoning LLMs. This is no longer statistics. The model (after pretraining, as in all examples above) tries to solve tasks and gets rewarded for correct answers. It learns to reason in its own idiosyncratic way. Almost every new model now goes through RL post-training.

2025 — Grok 4 Heavy. Runs several agents in parallel, each solving the task independently. The agents then "discuss" and reach consensus. I won't bother explaining why this is more than statistics.

LLMs aren't just advanced statistical machines. They were in 2022. Some people stopped studying them then. And these are only some of the key discoveries in the field.

If the "LLMs are just statistics" argument doesn't hold, it brings me back to this channel's main question: what's stopping them from writing texts with answers no human can give, or asking questions no human would think to ask?
